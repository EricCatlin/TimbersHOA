# Rules and Regulations
## The Timbers Condominium Association

These rules are set forth pursuant to the authority conferred to The Timbers Condominium Association, hereinafter referred to as the “Association." Authority conferred by Section 13.6.3 of that certain Declaration of Covenants, Conditions, and Restrictions for The Timbers Condominium Association recorded under Snohomish County Recording No. 201905225003 (as amended from time to time, the "CC&Rs”), which states "The Board shall have the power to adopt Rules to establish and enforce construction and design criteria and aesthetic standards pertaining to the improvements and alterations to the Community.”

### Intent

It is the intent of these rules and regulations to augment the recorded CC&Rs for the purpose of protecting the value and desirability of the homes within the Community and protecting the natural beauty of the common areas, which is the purpose of the Association.

These Community Rules and Regulations do not replace the Articles of Incorporation, CC&Rs, or Bylaws. Rather, they supplement those documents. To the extent these rules and regulations conflict with the CC&Rs or Bylaws, the CC&Rs or Bylaws shall govern.

Any violation of the rules and regulations below or the CC&Rs will be considered a finable offense. A violation notice will be issued for violations stating the timeframe for compliance, the specifics of the alleged offense, and the fine schedule. Owners and tenants are responsible at all times for the reasonable conduct of their family members and guests. Owners are responsible for notification to tenants of covenant restrictions and rules and regulations. Owners are responsible for notifying the Board immediately of any change in billing address.


# Good Citizenship

Being a good citizen within our community means respecting the safety, peace, and well-being of the neighborhood and your neighbors. It is about fostering a harmonious living environment where everyone can enjoy their home and surroundings.

- **A. Quiet Hours**
  - **Details:** Quiet hours are established from 10:00 PM to 7:00 AM daily.
  - **Justification:** These hours support the normal sleep patterns for the majority of residents, ensuring that everyone can rest undisturbed.

- **B. Prohibition of Noxious or Offensive Activities**
  - **Details:** Engaging in activities that produce excessive noise, emit smoke, spread debris, or otherwise negatively affect neighbors is prohibited.
  - **Justification:** Every resident deserves to find peace within their own home. The HOA promotes freedom but maintains that one's actions should not adversely impact others.

- **C. Safe Driving Practices**
  - **Details:** Dangerous driving and speeding by owners or their guests within the neighborhood are strictly forbidden.
  - **Justification:** While the HOA does not own the roads, it can set standards for resident behavior. Our community includes routes to local schools and is home to many young children, making safety our paramount concern.

- **D. Littering and Dumping**
  - **Details:** No littering or dumping of personal items, trash, or waste in parks, green belts, or streets is allowed.
  - **Justification:** Littering and dumping are disrespectful behaviors that burden others with cleanup responsibilities. Maintaining cleanliness is essential for the aesthetic and health of our community.

- **E. Weed and Pest Management**
  - **Details:** Each owner is responsible for managing weeds and pests on their property such as wasps, rodents, invasive plants etc...
  - **Justification:** Uncontrolled weeds and pests can spread to neighboring yards and community spaces, causing broader infestations and health hazards. The HOA will impose fines for neglected properties that contribute to this issue. Proper management is crucial to prevent the spread of pests like bees, wasps, and mosquitoes, which can pose risks beyond individual properties.

- **F. HOA Park Usage**
  - **Details:** Park usage is limited to HOA members and their guests. Users must ensure their activities do not cause damage to park equipment or grounds.  No Fires or Fireworks.
  - **Justification:** Vandalism or improper use of park facilities not only incurs costs for repairs but also detracts from the enjoyment and availability of these areas for all residents. It is vital that everyone treats park spaces with respect, ensuring they remain a valuable and enjoyable resource for the community.

# Parking and Vehicle Storage

To maintain the beauty, safety, and accessibility of our community, residents are expected to follow parking guidelines strictly.

- **A. Residential Parking**
  - **Details:** Residents are expected to use their driveways for parking, refraining from using the street as permanent parking. Each home is equipped with a 2-car garage and a driveway, accommodating a minimum of 4 vehicles.
  - **Justification:** This policy is in place to ensure streets remain clear for emergency vehicles, guest parking, and to maintain the aesthetic appeal of our neighborhood.

- **B. Fire Lanes and Sidewalks**
  - **Details:** Parking in fire lanes or in a manner that blocks sidewalks is strictly prohibited.
  - **Justification:** It is crucial to ensure that emergency vehicles have clear access at all times and that pedestrians can safely use sidewalks without obstructions.

- **C. Recreational Vehicle Storage**
  - **Details:** Storing recreational vehicles on the street is not permitted.
  - **Justification:** To preserve the visual harmony of our community and ensure streets are not congested, recreational vehicles must be stored off the street. A recreational vehicle that fits in your driveway and follows other rules is generally ok.

- **D. Parking or Vehicle Exceptions**
  - **Details:** Households with more than 4 vehicles, such as homes with 5+ adults, or temporary-extended vehicle parking can be requestedfrom the  HOA to receive an allowance for extra vehicles to park on the street on an as-needed basis.  
  - **Justification:** This policy allows the HOA to manage street parking efficiently while accommodating larger households. Long-term street-parked vehicles without approval may be subject to fines.

# Yard Maintenance

Maintaining the aesthetics and health of our community's outdoor spaces is essential. Yards are a visible reflection of our community’s pride and contribute to the overall beauty and ambiance of our neighborhood.

- **A. Yard Maintenance Standards**
  - **Details:** All front yard landscaping and any landscaping visible from the street or common areas must be well-maintained. This includes regular mowing, weed removal, fertilization, and watering—except in times of water rationing decreed by the local water utility. Yards can be comprised of grass and shrubs as originally delivered by the builder, artificial turf, or even hardscaped areas. Regardless of the chosen landscape style, it must be maintained to ensure it remains inoffensive and in good condition. Large alterations in appearance should be approved via architecture review process
  - **Justification:** Given the proximity of homes to each other, it’s important for the overall community aesthetic that individual yards are kept in a state that harmonizes with the neighborhood. While we permit a broad range of personal tastes and preferences in yard design, the key requirement is that yards should not detract from the community's overall appearance. We encourage residents to consider the prevailing styles of their neighbors to ensure a cohesive look throughout the community, though we do not enforce strict conformity of style.

# 4. Animals

Residents are permitted to keep pets, reflecting our community's appreciation for the companionship and joy they bring. However, to ensure the comfort and safety of all residents, specific guidelines govern pet ownership.

- **A. Pet Ownership Guidelines**
  - **Details:** Dogs, cats, and other indoor household pets are allowed within the community, adhering to the guidelines outlined in these Community Rules and Regulations and Section 10.2.8 of the CC&Rs. All animals must be kept primarily as domestic pets. The ownership of pets should not lead to excessive noise, odors, or other disturbances. Commercial breeding or maintaining an unreasonable number of pets is prohibited.
  - **Justification:** These rules ensure that pets contribute positively to the community environment without becoming a source of disruption or nuisance.

- **B. Public Spaces and Pet Conduct**
  - **Details:** Pets are welcome in community parks and outdoor spaces, provided they are controlled and accompanied by a responsible person at all times. Owners must promptly clean up any pet waste and dispose of it in appropriate containers. Pets should be controlled to prevent excessive noise or disturbances to neighbors and other community members.
  - **Justification:** Responsible pet ownership and conduct in public spaces are essential for maintaining cleanliness, safety, and harmony within the community. Immediate cleanup of pet waste is crucial for public health and environmental reasons.

- **C. Consideration for Neighbors**
  - **Details:** Pet owners must ensure their pets do not create excessive noise, odors, or other effects that could impede the comfort and enjoyment of other residents. Measures should be taken to minimize any potential disturbances, especially during quiet hours.
  - **Justification:** Mutual respect and consideration among residents are foundational to a harmonious community. These guidelines aim to balance the joys of pet ownership with the rights of all residents to enjoy a peaceful living environment.


#### 7. Home Business
- **A.** Home business occupations are permitted provided such businesses are undetectable from the street by sight, sound, odor, or noise.
  - **i.** The business use shall operate in its entirety within the dwelling unit and only by persons residing in the dwelling.
  - **ii.** The business use shall be clearly incidental and secondary to the use of the dwelling for dwelling purposes and shall not change the character of the dwelling or of the neighborhood by increasing noise, lights, traffic, or other disturbances.
  - **iii.** The business operation must comply with all applicable municipal ordinances, and maintain current licenses as required by the municipality. The Board will require copies of licenses.

#### 8. Signage (Section 10.2.7) With ACC approval
- **A.** No more than one professionally made “Home for Sale" or “Home for Lease" sign not larger than six (6) square feet in total size shall be displayed at any time.

#### 9. Architectural Changes (Section 11.5)
- **A.** No structure shall be constructed or caused to be constructed on any Unit unless the plans for the structure have been approved in writing by the ACC.
  - **i.** This includes any building, wall, or other structure or exterior addition, change, or alteration to it that is to be commenced or erected upon a Unit; including parking bays, new roofs or siding materials, sheds, garages, play structures, decks, and any other item that would be visible from the street or common areas.

#### 10. Satellite Dishes and Antennas
- **A.** Satellite dishes not exceeding twenty-four inches (24”) in diameter (or other size hereafter adopted by the Board or ACC to reasonably accommodate Owners, as set forth in Section 11.5.2.3 of the CC&Rs) may be allowed on the buildings with the prior written approval of the ACC. The satellite dish must also meet the following criteria:
  - **a.** The satellite dish is placed in the most discreet location.
  - **b.** The satellite is screened from view.
  - **c.** The satellite dish is not visible from the Street.

#### 11. Assessments, Late Fees, and Fines
- **A.** Homeowner assessments are due on the 1st of each month and are considered late if not received (postmarked) by the 5th of each month.
- **B.** A $25.00 late fee will be charged for any assessments or outstanding balances that are not paid within 5 days of the due date. In addition, a $10.00 fee may be assessed each time a late fee is charged to cover the administrative fees for collecting any late assessments.
- **C.** An additional $25.00 late fee will be charged for each subsequent month that payment is not made, or the account has an outstanding balance owing. In addition, a $10.00 fee is charged to cover the administrative fees for collecting any late assessments.
- **D.** If a homeowner's account has an overdue balance for 90 days or more, or if otherwise determined necessary by the Board, the Association may bring action against the person or entity per the governing documents. Said action may include all or any one of the following actions:
  - Record a lien for the amount of the assessment plus interest, late charges, any attorney fees and costs incurred or estimated to be incurred in enforcing the lien with the County within which the residence is located.
  - Setting up a personal judgment
  - Garnishment of wages and accounts
  - Judicial foreclosure
  - Non-judicial foreclosure
  - Sending account to a collection agency

#### 12. Violations and Fines
- **A.** The first violation of the Rules and Regulations or the CC&Rs will result in a written warning.
- **B.** The second violation notice of the same or similar Rule or Section of the CC&Rs within 12 months will result in a $50 fine.
- **C.** The third violation notice of the same or similar Rule or Section of the CC&Rs within 12 months will result in a $100 fine.
- **D.** The fourth violation notice of the same or similar Rule or Section of the CC&Rs within 12 months will result in a $200 fine.
- **E.** Each subsequent violation notice for infraction of the same or similar Rule or Section of the CC&Rs within 12 months will result in a $200 fine.

#### 13. Appeal Process
- **A.** If a homeowner feels that a notice of violation is not legitimate, or feels that clarification of the violation is necessary, they may submit an appeal in writing to the Board in care of the HOA Management Company within 48 hours after the notice of violation is received. The Board will have 30 days to review and issue a ruling.
- **B.** As part of their appeal submission or during the review process, the homeowner may submit any evidence, statements, and other information concerning the violation. The homeowner may also request to be heard before the Board prior to their determination. The Board shall consider all reasonable information and shall grant any request to be heard prior to their ruling.
  - **i.** If the ruling on the appeal is in favor of the homeowner, then a notice of resolution will be issued to the homeowner.
  - **ii.** If the ruling on the appeal is not in favor of the homeowner, the homeowner is subject to the aforementioned fine schedule and the 12-month violation time period will begin to accrue again from the point at which the violation was issued.
